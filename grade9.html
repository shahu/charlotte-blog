Grade 9: Science & Technology

Click on each topic to see more details, including related AP course knowledge points and resources.

The Material World

Quebec Learning Requirements:

Key concepts include characteristic properties (e.g., mass, volume, temperature), states of matter, acidity/alkalinity, physical and chemical changes, conservation of matter, mixtures, solutions, and separation techniques. These concepts lay the foundation for understanding material interactions and transformations.

Details

The Living World

Quebec Learning Requirements:

This area covers habitats, ecological niches, species, populations, physical and behavioral adaptations, evolution, taxonomy, genetics (genes, chromosomes), reproduction (asexual and sexual, including mechanisms in plants and animals), human development stages, contraception, sexually transmitted diseases, and cellular processes like photosynthesis, respiration, osmosis, and diffusion. It also includes characteristics of living things and cell structure visible under a microscope, providing a comprehensive view of life sciences.

Details

Earth and Space

Quebec Learning Requirements:

Key concepts include the internal structure of Earth (lithosphere, hydrosphere, atmosphere), types of rocks and minerals, atmospheric layers, water distribution, air composition, soil types, geological processes (tectonic plates, volcanoes, earthquakes, orogenesis, erosion), natural energy sources, the water cycle, renewable and nonrenewable resources, Universal Gravitation (qualitative study), solar system components, light properties, celestial cycles (day/night, moon phases, eclipses, seasons), and phenomena like comets, aurora borealis, and meteoroid impacts. This area connects Earth sciences with astronomical understanding.

Details

The Technological World

Quebec Learning Requirements:

This area focuses on specifications, design plans, technical drawings, manufacturing processes, raw materials, equipment, systems (functions, inputs, processes, outputs, control), components, basic mechanical functions (links, guiding control), energy transformations, types of motion, effects of forces, simple machines, and mechanisms for transmitting or changing motion. It equips students with skills for technological design and engineering principles.
